The goal of this guide is simple: get one of your devices onto [siliconwit.io](https://siliconwit.io), see its data live, and then make the platform do something useful with it, send you an alert and act on its own. You can do the whole thing with a single sensor, and you do not need to commit anything to begin. #IoT #GettingStarted #DeviceMonitoring

## From Zero to a Live Device

<InArticleAd />


<Steps>

1. **Create a free account.** Sign up at [siliconwit.io](https://siliconwit.io) and open the dashboard. The free tier covers your first few devices, so there is nothing to pay while you learn the platform.

2. **Add a device and get its token.** From the dashboard, add a device. The platform issues a unique, per-device token so each device authenticates on its own. You choose how it will connect on the next step.

3. **Connect it.** Two common paths:
   - **Use a pre-built firmware image** for a common board (ESP32, Raspberry Pi). The credentials are embedded, so you flash and go without editing code on day one.
   - **Use any standard client.** Point an existing MQTT, MQTT-SN, CoAP, or HTTP client at the endpoints listed on [mqtt.siliconwit.io](https://mqtt.siliconwit.io). The connection is TLS-encrypted and uses your device token.

   It works the same across WiFi, cellular, NB-IoT, and LoRaWAN networks.

4. **See it live.** Your device appears on the dashboard with its readings updating in real time. Add a chart for the values you care about; this is the "see everything, live" view your whole team can read.

</Steps>

## Make the Data Work for You

<InArticleAd />


A live reading is useful; a reading that warns you and acts on its own is the point.

<Steps>

1. **Set an alert.** Choose a reading and a threshold (a temperature ceiling, a tank level, a vibration limit) and pick how you want to hear about it: email, SMS, Slack, Discord, or a webhook. Now you find out the moment something drifts, not hours later.

2. **Add an automation rule.** Go one step further than being told: have the platform respond. A rule can toggle a relay, change a setpoint, or send a command back to the device when a condition is met, with an approval step where a human should stay in the loop.

3. **Let AI help.** On the higher tiers, AI analytics flag anomalies and forecast trends, and the MCP integration lets you ask an assistant, in plain language, what your devices are doing and have it set alerts or change rules for you.

</Steps>
